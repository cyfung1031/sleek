Welcome to my website! I am a Ph.D. student in Dr. Nathan Kraft's Plant Community Ecology Lab in the Department of Ecology and Evolutionary Biology at UCLA. I received my B.S. in Biology at Brown University, where I was a member of Dr. Dov Sax's Conservation Science Lab. My research interests are broadly in plant community ecology and biogeography. Some topics that I am interested in exploring include:

  1. The relative importane of different fundamental ecological processes (e.g. selection, ecological drift, dispersal) in determining community dynamics along environmental gradients and across spatial scales.
  2. How local coexistence mechanisms and regional community assembly processes can interact with one another to jointly influence community structure and dynamics.
  3. How functional or demographic information can be used to better understand species' large-scale environmental tolerances (i.e. climatic niches) and geographic distributions.
I aim to explore these topics from the perspectives of functional traits, niche concepts, population dynamics, and metacommunity processes. Stay tuned as my research develops!
